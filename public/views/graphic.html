How to Cook Juicy Chicken

1. Thin the Meat

A uniform thickness allows the meat to cook evenly. This can be done by cutting the chicken in half or by pounding it into a thinner shape.

Thinning the meat will also help reduce the cooking time thus allowing less water to evaporate out of the chicken.

2. Season

Season with salt and flavor with any spices, marinades, sauces, or herb mixes. Salt helps the chicken retain moisture and other spices help add flavor.

A simple Italian seasoning consists of a blend of basil, oregano, rosemary, and thyme.

3. Cook to 155 ℉ Internal

Cook at a high heat until the chicken reaches 155 ℉ in the middle. Let rest for one minute without cutting. A high heat helps the chicken develop a flavorful crust that will secure the inner juices.

Allowing the meat to rest for one minute at 155 ℉ internal kills the same amount of bacteria as cooking to 165 ℉.
